I am currently a master’s student at Johns Hopkins, specializing in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) combined with biofluid mechanics under the guidance of Professor Rajat Mittal. I earned my B.S. degree in Theoretical and Applied Mechanics from the Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech).

During 2022-2023, I spent my year as a full-time visiting student at MIT, where I achieved full marks in all courses and made a contribution to the work “Intraspecific Diversity in Thermal Performance Determines Phytoplankton Ecological Niche” in Professor Mick Follows’ Lab.

My research interests lie in computational fluid dynamics, biofluids, and scientific computing, with the goal of developing innovative computer-based simulation techniques to simulate turbulent flows and multiphase flows in different cases in engineering.
